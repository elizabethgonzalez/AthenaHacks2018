Pancakes vs Waffles

A light, fluffy treat goes up against a spongy, pourous delight.
Both are staples of American culture, but which breakfast bliss will take the cake?

Pancakes: America's Sweetheart

Pancakes have long been a tradition in the US of A, and generations have grown up savoring the crepe's thicc cousin with a generous helping of that sweet Aunt Jemima. A pro of pancakes is that they come in a diverse array of flavors, shapes, and sizes. Anyone can enjoy this sweet treat, from babies to grandmas. From mini pancakes to jumbo pancakes to blueberry pancakes to chocolate chips pancakes...pancakes.

Pancakes are cool.

Waffles: America's Naughty Crush

Waffles are relative newcomers to the US of A breakfast scene, being brought over by our culinary betters, the Belgians, in 1843. These bad boys have been around long enough for our mommies and daddies to mildly dissapprove of the nutricious food we just can't get enough of. Waffles are a little more rigid with their structure, with less room for play and experimentation, but they are decidedly easier to make with the invention of the sweet sweet wafflemaker.

Waffles are badass.
